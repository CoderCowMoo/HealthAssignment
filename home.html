Stigma and Discrimination Concerning Mental Health


Definitions

Stigma: Is when family or friends see one negatively if they have talked to them about mental illness or depression and anxiety.

Stigma is an entirely negative behaviour of cultures past, meant to reserve strength. It has been discovered that mental health and the discussion of it is helpful in dealing with illnesses such as depression and anxiety. Stigma is harmful for those suffering from it and community as a whole. The victim of stigma often feels shameful and isolated from the community around them. They feel self-doubt and a lack of self-confidence. This leads to less opportunities for employment or social interaction. Unfortunately this can sometimes lead to suicide from the helplessness. As it can be seen, stigma merely makes the problem worse and no solutions can arrise from pressure.
